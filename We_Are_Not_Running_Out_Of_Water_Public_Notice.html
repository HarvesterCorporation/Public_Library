We Are Not Running Out Of Water

This page presents a public environmental clarification regarding global water systems, reflecting Harvester Corporation’s commitment to preservation, stewardship, and continuity.

Overview

Earth has a fixed amount of water. It always has.

Every drop that has ever fallen as rain, flowed in a river, frozen in a glacier, or lived inside a human body is still here today — just moving between air, land, and sea.

Climate change is not destroying water. It is moving it.

When polar ice melts, the water does not vanish. It enters the oceans. When forests are cut, rain does not disappear — it stops falling in the places that need it.

The Water Cycle as a Living System

Water moves through:

  • clouds
  • forests
  • soil
  • rivers
  • animals
  • people

These living systems are what turn vapor into rain and rain into life. When we damage them, water no longer returns where it should.

Misunderstanding Extremes

Drought is not “lack of water.” It is failure of circulation.

Floods are not “too much water.” They are water with nowhere to go.

Human Responsibility

Human survival depends not on how much water exists — but on whether we protect the living systems that move it.

We must stop treating Earth as a mine and return to being its guardians.

Conclusion

Because the water has never left. Only our care for it has.
